Are LLMs gender biased toward occupation?

Gender Disparities and Stereotypes in Professional Roles. Despite strides in gender equality, biases persist in occupational choices. Men are often favored for higher-status managerial positions, while women are more commonly relegated to mid-level managerial roles. Additionally, societal assumptions lead to gendered expectations, with women stereotypically directed towards nurturing professions like nursing and teaching, perpetuating entrenched gender roles.

In the context of occupational bias and gender disparities, there is a risk that LLMs may reflect and reinforce existing biases found in society. If the training data contains examples of gendered language or stereotypes related to occupation, LLMs may learn and reproduce those biases in their generated text.

I conducted a comparative analysis between ChatGPT-3.5 and Claude-2.1 on https://chat.lmsys.org/ to evaluate the extent of gender bias in their responses regarding occupations.

It’s not uncommon for individuals to have multiple jobs or occupations to support their families or pursue their interests. If you have any specific questions or concerns related to your family situation, feel free to share more details, and I’ll do my best to help.

In conclusion, the LLMs don’t necessarily assume that women or men cannot perform certain jobs. However, they do tend to associate certain occupations with specific genders, reflecting societal stereotypes and biases.
