Creating a good GPT prompt involves clarity, context, specificity, and structure. Here are some guidelines to help you craft effective prompts:

1. Be Clear and Concise

  • Specific Request: Clearly state what you want. Avoid ambiguous language.
    • Example: Instead of “Tell me about AI,” say “Explain the history and development of artificial intelligence from the 1950s to the present.”

2. Provide Context

  • Background Information: Give enough context so the model understands the scope of the request.
    • Example: “For a presentation to high school students, explain the basic concepts of machine learning in simple terms.”

3. Ask Direct Questions

  • Focused Queries: Ask specific questions to get precise answers.
    • Example: “What are the main differences between supervised and unsupervised learning?”

4. Set the Tone and Style

  • Desired Style: Specify the tone, style, or format you want.
    • Example: “Write a humorous story about a day in the life of a robot.”

5. Use Examples

  • Illustrate Requests: Provide examples to clarify your needs.
    • Example: “Generate a professional email response to a job offer, similar to this: [include example].”

6. Limit the Scope

  • Specificity: Narrow down broad topics to specific aspects.
    • Example: Instead of “Discuss climate change,” say “Explain the impact of climate change on polar bear populations.”

7. Use Proper Formatting

  • Organized Prompts: Break down complex requests into bullet points or numbered lists.
    • Example:
      1. “Describe the main features of Python programming language.”
      2. “Give examples of its applications in data science.”

Examples of Good Prompts

  1. Descriptive Prompt
    • “Describe the economic impact of the COVID-19 pandemic on small businesses in the United States.”
  2. Creative Prompt
    • “Write a short sci-fi story about humans colonizing Mars and encountering alien life.”
  3. Technical Prompt
    • “Explain the difference between deterministic and stochastic models in operations research.”
  4. Instructive Prompt
    • “Provide a step-by-step guide on how to set up a WordPress blog for beginners.”
  5. Comparative Prompt
    • “Compare and contrast the leadership styles of Steve Jobs and Elon Musk.”

By following these guidelines, you can create effective GPT prompts that yield informative and relevant responses.

Then I prompt a criteria of score 0-10 to evaluate how good a prompt is.

Evaluating the quality of a prompt can be done using a set of criteria, each scored on a scale from 0 to 10. Here are the criteria with descriptions:

1. Clarity

  • Description: Is the prompt clear and easy to understand?
  • Score 0-10: 0 (very confusing) to 10 (perfectly clear)

2. Context

  • Description: Does the prompt provide enough background information for the model to understand the scope?
  • Score 0-10: 0 (no context) to 10 (excellent context)

3. Specificity

  • Description: Is the prompt specific enough to get a precise response?
  • Score 0-10: 0 (very vague) to 10 (highly specific)

4. Directness

  • Description: Does the prompt ask direct questions or make direct requests?
  • Score 0-10: 0 (very indirect) to 10 (very direct)

5. Tone and Style

  • Description: Does the prompt specify the desired tone, style, or format?
  • Score 0-10: 0 (no tone/style specified) to 10 (tone/style clearly specified)

6. Examples

  • Description: Does the prompt provide examples to illustrate the request?
  • Score 0-10: 0 (no examples) to 10 (excellent examples provided)

7. Scope Limitation

  • Description: Does the prompt narrow down broad topics to specific aspects?
  • Score 0-10: 0 (very broad) to 10 (well-defined scope)

8. Formatting

  • Description: Is the prompt well-organized, using bullet points or lists where necessary?
  • Score 0-10: 0 (poorly formatted) to 10 (well-organized)

9. Relevance

  • Description: Is the prompt relevant to the intended use or audience?
  • Score 0-10: 0 (not relevant) to 10 (highly relevant)

10. Creativity (if applicable)

  • Description: Does the prompt encourage creative or unique responses (for creative tasks)?
  • Score 0-10: 0 (not creative) to 10 (very creative)

Please give me three example prompts to generate a post about LLMs

Here are three example prompts to generate a post about Large Language Models (LLMs):

Prompt 1: Informative Overview

“Write a detailed post explaining what Large Language Models (LLMs) are, including their history, development, key features, and applications in various industries. Make sure to highlight the significance of LLMs in modern AI research and provide examples of popular LLMs such as GPT-4, BERT, and T5.”

Prompt 2: Technical Deep Dive

“Create a technical blog post that delves into the architecture and functioning of Large Language Models (LLMs). Discuss how LLMs are trained, the underlying neural network structures, and the challenges faced during their development. Include discussions on concepts like transformers, attention mechanisms, and fine-tuning, using examples from well-known models like GPT-4 and BERT.”

Prompt 3: Impact and Ethical Considerations

“Write a comprehensive post discussing the impact of Large Language Models (LLMs) on society and the ethical considerations associated with their use. Cover topics such as data privacy, potential biases in LLMs, and the implications for various sectors like healthcare, education, and entertainment. Include real-world examples and potential solutions to address ethical challenges.”

These prompts provide a clear focus, context, and specific aspects to cover, ensuring that the generated posts are informative, relevant, and engaging.
